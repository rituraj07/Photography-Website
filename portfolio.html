Protrait Photography


Get a Modal

Modal means someone of whome you would take shots. A good place to begin is with family or friends, as your relationship means that you are not dealing with strangers. From them you can get genuine feedback about the shots.

  • Aperture: f/1.4
  • ISO: 100
  • Shutter: 1/160s

Change Your Perspective

Most of the protraits are shot from eye level. Protraits are the major focused on models face and people use protraits to showcase their facial features. If shot from a different angle and different level protrait may look unique and attractive. Try to think different and change your viewpoint.

  • Aperture: f/1.6
  • ISO: 200
  • Shutter: 1/320s

Eye Contact is Essintial

Sometimes eyecontact of modal with the camera gives a very intense and deep feeling to the photograph. A good eyecontact gives very natural feel to shot, but sometimes not having an eye contact is much better then having an eyecontact. This can create a feeling of candidness and also create a little intrigue and interest as the viewer of the shot wonders what they are looking at.

  • Aperture: f/4.0
  • ISO: 400
  • Shutter: 1/40s

Play with Lighting

Lighting is the most important aspect of Photography. In pritrait shot lighting can change the mood of photograph. Having less light in the shot gives intense mood to shot where as bright light express joy. Experimenting with the lighting is the best thing you can do in protrait shots.

  • Aperture: f/11.0
  • ISO: 125
  • Shutter: 1/160s

Choose your portrait lens

Protrait lens are those lens which have constant focal lenth i.e. they have constant zoom. Benefit with these type of lens is that they offer a very good aperture value so that the background is highly blur and face of model is highly focused. These type of lens are mainly used for protrait shots.

  • Aperture: f/5.6
  • ISO: 400
  • Shutter: 1/60s

Shoot candid shots

For me best shots are candid shots whenever I shoot candid shots mostly I end up having good shots. Candid shots are shots that are taken without informing the model .It is best shot because it is very natural, sometimes you may require Telephoto lens to get candid shots from far distance.

  • Aperture: f/7.1
  • ISO: 800
  • Shutter: 1/250s
